# Moto Flash Express Funtional Requirements

1. Client Module (App/Web)

- User Registration: The system must allow registration via email, phone number, or social media.

- Profile Management: Users shall be able to edit their name, phone number, and payment methods.

- Errand Request: The system must allow users to describe personal errands (e.g., pharmacy purchases).

- Delivery Request: The system must allow the delivery of documents or small packages.

- Automatic Geolocation: The system must detect the client's current location using the device's GPS.

- Fare Calculation: The system must automatically calculate costs based on a base fare of $40 MXN plus additional kilometers.

- Order History: Clients must be able to view a list of previous services along with their respective receipts.

- Real-Time Tracking: The system must display the driver's location on a map during the service.

- Push Notifications: Sending alerts regarding order status (assigned, en route, delivered).

- Internal Chat: Direct and secure communication between the client and the assigned driver.

- Payment Methods: The system must process payments via credit/debit cards, digital wallets, and cash.

- Favorites: Option to save frequent addresses (home, office, etc.).

- Rating and Reviews: The client will be able to evaluate the service and the driver after the delivery is completed.

- Scheduled Deliveries: The system must allow scheduling a service for a future date and time.

- Order Cancellation: Users may cancel an order before the driver reaches the pickup point.

- Discount Coupons: Validation and application of promotional codes to the order total.

- File Upload: Allied businesses must be able to upload files (PDF/XML) for their invoices.

2. Driver Module (Driver App)

- Service Acceptance: Drivers must be able to accept or reject errand requests.

- Availability Status: A button to mark themselves as "Active" or "Inactive."

- GPS Navigation: Integration with external maps (Google Maps/Waze) for optimized routing.

- Proof of Delivery: Drivers must be able to upload a photograph or digital signature as evidence of delivery.

- Earnings Summary: Visualization of generated daily and weekly earnings.

- Errand Details: Access to specific instructions provided by the client.

- Proximity Alert: The system will automatically notify the client when the driver is within 500 meters.

- Panic Button: Direct link to emergency services or technical support in case of incidents.

- Tip Management: The system must allow drivers to view digital tips received.

- Data Consumption: Drivers will be able to see a report of the app's mobile data usage.

3. Administrative Module (Dashboard)

- Driver Management: Administrators can register or remove drivers and their vehicles.

- Fleet Monitoring: A general map showing the real-time location of all active motorcycles.

- Fare Configuration: Administrators can modify base prices and per-kilometer charges.

- Conciliation Panel: Report of total income minus operating costs (gasoline, maintenance).

- Partner Management: Registration and control of local businesses with preferential rates.

- KPI Reports: Generation of reports on average delivery times and customer satisfaction.

- Incident Management: Tool to resolve complaints regarding lost items or late deliveries.

- Maintenance Audit: Log of technical service dates for each motorcycle.

- Shift Control: Assignment of work schedules to cover 7 days a week.

4. Business and Logistics Requirements

- Cash on Delivery: The system must support the option to charge the client for the product at the time of delivery.

- Business Service: A specific module for pharmacies or restaurants to request bulk deliveries.

- Purchase Management: Drivers must be able to enter the amount spent on grocery purchases for reimbursement by the client.

- Digital Visual Identity: The interface must respect the institutional colors (Red, Black, White) and the official logo.
